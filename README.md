# Viable Dahlia's Load Order

Hello, I'm Dahlia <img src="assets/viable-dahlia-avatar.png" width="20" height="20" alt="Viable Dahlia avatar" style="border-radius: 50%;" />

Here you'll find the mods behind every [Fallout 4 Anniversary Edition](https://fallout.bethesda.net/en/games/fallout-4/welcome-home) (AE) build on my channel - 🎮[ViableDahlia](https://www.youtube.com/@ViableDahlia) 🎮 (all about architectural settlement building.) <sup>AE = 1.11.191 for my nerds</sup>

**Why this page?** My builds share a common load order, with small documented changes to suit their themes. That's for two reasons:

1️⃣ **Variety**: Sometimes I'm building a solarpunk settlement, others, a cyberpunk dystopia, and heck, even a western-themed commonwealth. I like my settlements to *feel* lived in, which means ✨**mods**✨, and neatly brings me to reason two️.

2️⃣ **Simplicity**: I shy away from large mods that give you *everything*. Great job from mod authors but finding stuff is my build menu nightmare! So, I opt for smaller, more focused mods that add features the base game doesn't support, like flags fluttering in the breeze, NPCs visiting, and thematic art & decor. 

Since I prefer not clutter my load order, I use [Mod Organizer 2](https://www.modorganizer.org/) for thematic build [profiles](https://youtu.be/tadcXfpZPAs?si=KbIFdCSASzFb_46F). Yes, it's extra, *yes*, I'm neurodivergent, why did you ask? In any case, everything is shared here so you can follow along, replicate a build, or adapt it for your own playstyle.

## Foundation

My base load order is built on **[The Midnight Ride](https://themidnightride.moddinglinked.com/)**, a stability-focused modding guide for Fallout 4.

I made two adjustments:

🛑 Included [base finish](https://themidnightride.moddinglinked.com/basefinish.html) mods that support Anniversary Edition

🛑 Excluded minor fixes and [extended finish](https://themidnightride.moddinglinked.com/finish.html) mods I won't use

> New to modding? **[The Midnight Ride](https://themidnightride.moddinglinked.com/)** is a great guide to understand *why* a load order is structured the way it is before diving in. I especially love [Safe Modding](https://themidnightride.moddinglinked.com/safe-modding.html).
